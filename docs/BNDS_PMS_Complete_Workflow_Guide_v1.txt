1. Prescription Intake & Fill Process

This section covers the complete lifecycle of a prescription from the moment it enters the BNDS
PMS through final dispensing to the patient. The system supports multiple intake channels and
guides each prescription through a structured workflow to ensure accuracy, safety, and
regulatory compliance.

1.1 Prescription Entry Channels

Prescriptions enter the system through one of five channels. Each source is tracked for
reporting and audit purposes.

    À eRx via SureScripts -- Electronic prescriptions received directly from prescriber EMR
         systems

    À Prescriber Portal -- Prescriptions submitted by providers through the BNDS web portal
    À Walk-in -- Paper prescriptions presented at the counter and manually entered by staff
    À Phone/Fax -- Prescriptions called in or faxed by the prescriber's office
    À Patient Portal -- Refill requests submitted by patients online

System Action (Automated)

When an eRx or portal prescription arrives, the system automatically creates an Intake Queue entry,
attempts patient matching by name/DOB/phone, and assigns a priority level (Normal, Urgent, or
STAT).

1.2 Intake Queue Processing

All incoming prescriptions land in the Intake Queue where they await review and processing by
pharmacy staff.

    1. Staff opens the Intake Queue from the sidebar navigation.
    2. Each entry shows: patient name, prescriber, medication, source, priority badge, and

         current status.
    3. Staff reviews the prescription details for completeness and clinical appropriateness.
    4. If the patient is not already in the system, staff creates a new patient record with

         demographics, allergies, insurance, and phone numbers.
    5. Staff matches the prescription to an existing patient record using MRN, name, or phone

         lookup.
    6. Staff verifies insurance eligibility and selects the appropriate plan for billing.
    7. The prescription is moved from Intake to the appropriate workflow queue (typically Print).

  Staff Role: Pharmacy Technician
  Reviews intake entries, creates/matches patient records, verifies insurance, and advances
  prescriptions into the workflow queue.

1.3 Adjudication & Claims Processing

Before a prescription can be filled, the system submits a claim to the patient's insurance for
adjudication.

    1. The system builds an NCPDP claim transaction with drug NDC, quantity, days supply,
         prescriber NPI, and patient insurance details.

    2. The claim is submitted electronically to the payer/PBM.
    3. The system receives a response: Paid, Rejected, or Pending.
    4. If Paid: the copay amount is stored on the fill record. The prescription advances to Print.
    5. If Rejected: the prescription moves to the Reject queue with the rejection code and

         message. Staff reviews and resolves (e.g., prior auth, DAW change, plan override).
    6. If Pending: the prescription remains in an adjudicating state until a response is received.

System Action (Automated)

Claims are submitted automatically when a prescription enters the adjudication stage. Rejection codes
are parsed and displayed with human-readable descriptions. Claim history is logged for audit.

1.4 Label Printing & Fill

    1. Staff selects a prescription from the Print queue.
    2. The system generates a label with: patient name, medication name/strength, directions

         (SIG), quantity, refills remaining, prescriber, pharmacy info, lot number, expiration, and
         required warnings.
    3. The label prints to the configured Zebra or Dymo label printer.
    4. The technician selects the correct drug stock from inventory, verifies the NDC barcode
         against the prescription record, counts or measures the medication, and affixes the
         label.
    5. The prescription advances to the Scan queue.

  Staff Role: Pharmacy Technician
  Prints labels, selects drug stock, performs NDC verification scan, counts/measures medication, and
  affixes labels to containers.

1.5 Scan & Pharmacist Verification

    1. Staff scans the filled prescription's barcode at the Scan station.
    2. The system displays the prescription details alongside the scanned product for visual

         comparison.
    3. The prescription moves to the Verify queue.

4. The pharmacist (RPh) reviews the filled prescription: correct drug, correct strength,
    correct quantity, correct patient, correct label, and no drug interactions or clinical
    concerns.

5. The pharmacist either approves (moves to Waiting Bin) or rejects (sends back to Print
    with notes).

Staff Role: Pharmacist (RPh)

Performs final verification: drug/dose/patient check, DUR review, clinical judgment. This step is legally
required and cannot be performed by technicians.

1.6 Waiting Bin & Patient Pickup

    1. Verified prescriptions are assigned a physical bin location (e.g., A1, B3, Z12) and placed
         in the waiting bin area.

    2. The system tracks how long each prescription has been in the bin. Items over 14 days
         are flagged as overdue.

    3. When the patient arrives, staff searches by name, Rx number, or MRN on the Pickup
         screen.

    4. Staff collects the copay via the POS module (cash, credit card, or insurance-only).
    5. The patient signs for receipt (via signature pad if configured).
    6. The prescription status changes to Sold. The fill is complete.

  System Action (Automated)
  The system sends an automated notification (email/SMS) to the patient when their prescription enters
  the waiting bin, informing them it is ready for pickup. Overdue items trigger return-to-stock (RTS)
  workflows after the configured threshold.

2. Compounding & Drug Manufacturing

This section describes how custom-compounded medications are created within the PMS, from
formula design through batch production and quality release. The compounding module
maintains full traceability for every batch produced.

2.1 Formula Creation & Management

    1. Navigate to Compounding > Formulas > New Formula.
    2. Enter the formula name, formula code, dosage form (cream, capsule, suspension, etc.),

         and route of administration.
    3. Add each ingredient: select from the inventory catalog, specify the quantity and unit of

         measure, and mark whether it is an active or inactive ingredient.
    4. Define compounding instructions as step-by-step text entries (e.g., "Weigh Active A to

         5.0g", "Levigate with base", "Mix 10 minutes at medium speed").
    5. Set the default Beyond-Use Date (BUD) in days based on USP <795>/<797> stability

         data.
    6. Save the formula. A version 1.0 is automatically created.

Staff Role: Pharmacist (RPh)

Creates and approves all formulas. Only pharmacists can modify active formulas or create new
versions.

2.2 Batch Production

    1. Navigate to Compounding > Batches > New Batch.
    2. Select the formula and version. Enter the desired batch quantity and unit.
    3. The system generates a unique batch number in the format BYYYYMMDD-### (e.g.,

         B20260416-001).
    4. The system calculates scaled ingredient quantities based on the batch size.
    5. The compounder weighs/measures each ingredient. If a pharmacy scale is connected,

         the measured weight is captured automatically via the Web Serial API.
    6. The compounder records the lot number and expiration date for each ingredient used

         (for full traceability).
    7. The compounder follows each compounding step, marking each as complete.
    8. The batch status changes to Completed.

  Staff Role: Pharmacy Technician (Compounder)
  Performs the physical compounding: weighing, mixing, encapsulating, or preparing the dosage form
  according to the formula instructions.

2.3 Quality Assurance & Release

    1. The pharmacist opens the completed batch record for QA review.

    2. The pharmacist verifies: correct formula version, correct ingredient quantities, lot
         numbers recorded, all steps completed, visual/physical inspection acceptable.

    3. The pharmacist assigns or confirms the Beyond-Use Date.

    4. If QA passes: the batch status changes to Verified, then Released. The batch is now
         available for dispensing.

    5. If QA fails: the batch is marked as Failed with a reason. The batch is quarantined and
         cannot be dispensed.

    6. Labels are printed for the batch containers with batch number, formula name, BUD,
         storage conditions, and lot info.

Staff Role: Pharmacist (RPh)

Performs QA verification, assigns BUD, and releases or rejects the batch. This is a legally required
pharmacist-only function.

3. Hardware Integration

The PMS supports direct integration with pharmacy hardware devices through the browser's
Web Serial API and standard USB/network protocols. All hardware configuration is managed
from Settings > Hardware.

3.1 Pharmacy Scales

    1. Connect the scale to the workstation via USB or RS-232 serial cable.
    2. Navigate to Settings > Hardware > Scales.
    3. Click "Add Scale" and select the serial port from the browser's device picker (Web Serial

         API).
    4. Configure the baud rate, data bits, and protocol to match the scale manufacturer

         (common: 9600 baud, 8N1).
    5. Test the connection by placing a known weight on the scale and verifying the reading

         appears in the PMS.
    6. Once configured, weights are automatically captured during compounding batch

         production when the compounder clicks "Read Scale" at each ingredient step.

3.2 Label Printers

The PMS generates prescription labels in standard pharmacy label formats compatible with
Zebra and Dymo thermal printers.

    À Zebra printers: Connect via USB or network. Configure in Settings > Hardware > Label
         Printers. Select the printer model, label width (standard 4), and print density.

    À Dymo printers: Connect via USB. The system auto-detects Dymo LabelWriter models.
    À Labels include: patient name, medication, SIG, quantity, refills, prescriber, warnings,

         barcode, and pharmacy information.

3.3 Barcode Scanners

Barcode scanners are used for NDC verification during the fill process and for patient lookup at
pickup.

    À Any USB HID barcode scanner works out of the box (keyboard wedge mode).
    À Scan an NDC barcode during the fill process to verify the correct drug product matches

         the prescription.
    À Scan a prescription barcode at the pickup counter to quickly locate the patient's order.

3.4 Receipt Printers & Signature Pads

    À Receipt printers: Configured in Settings > Hardware > Receipt Printers. Used at the POS
         for transaction receipts.

4. Reports & Analytics

The Reports and Analytics modules provide pharmacy leadership with operational intelligence,
performance metrics, and compliance data. Reports can be filtered by date range and exported
to CSV or PDF.

4.1 Available Reports

    À Daily Fill Report -- All prescriptions filled on a given date, with patient, drug, quantity,
         copay, and fill technician.

    À Rx Volume Trends -- Bar/line charts showing prescription volume over time (7 days, 30
         days, 90 days, YTD).

    À Revenue by Category -- Breakdown of revenue by payment type (insurance, cash,
         copay) with dollar amounts and percentages.

    À Top Drugs Dispensed -- Ranked list of the most-filled medications by fill count and
         revenue.

    À Turnaround Times -- Average time prescriptions spend in each workflow stage (intake
         to sold).

    À Claim Acceptance Rate -- Percentage of claims accepted vs. rejected, with top rejection
         codes.

    À Technician Productivity -- Fills per technician per day, ranked by volume.
    À Patient Metrics -- New patients, active/inactive counts, active ratio, and top patients by

         Rx count.
    À Compounding Batch Log -- All batches produced with formula, quantity, compounder,

         verifier, and QA status.
    À Inventory Report -- Current stock levels, expiration alerts, low-stock items, and reorder

         status.

4.2 Analytics Dashboard

The Analytics Dashboard (accessible from the Analytics tab) provides a unified view of all KPIs:
    8. Select a date range using the period selector (7d, 30d, 90d, YTD).
    9. Review the KPI summary cards: Total Fills, Revenue, Average Fills/Day, Claim
         Acceptance Rate, Active Patients.
    10. Scroll through interactive sections: Dispensing Trends, Revenue Breakdown, Top 10
         Drugs, Claims Performance, Payer Mix, Productivity, Patient Metrics, and Compounding.
    11. Export any report to CSV or PDF using the Export button on each section.

  Staff Role: Pharmacist / Manager
  Reviews analytics dashboards and reports. Only users with the "reports:read" permission can access
  the Analytics and Reports modules.

7. Compounding Processes (Detailed)

This section provides a detailed reference for the compounding module's advanced features
including formula versioning, lot tracking, batch numbering, and compliance documentation.

7.1 Formula Versioning

Every formula change creates a new version. This maintains a complete audit trail:

    À Version 1.0 is created when the formula is first saved.
    À Editing a formula (adding/removing ingredients, changing quantities, updating

         instructions) creates a new version (1.1, 1.2, etc.).
    À Previous versions remain accessible for historical reference.
    À Batches are always linked to a specific formula version, so you can trace exactly what

         recipe was used.

7.2 Ingredient Lot Tracking

For every ingredient used in a batch, the system records:

    À The ingredient item from the PMS inventory catalog.
    À The lot number of the physical stock used.
    À The expiration date of that lot.
    À The quantity used (and the actual measured weight if captured from a connected scale).
This enables full forward and backward traceability: from a batch to its ingredients, or from an
ingredient lot to all batches that used it.

7.3 Batch Numbering

Batches are automatically numbered using the format BYYYYMMDD-### where:

    À B = fixed prefix indicating a compounding batch
    À YYYYMMDD = the date the batch was created
    À ### = a sequential number that increments per day (001, 002, 003...)
Example: B20260416-003 is the third batch created on April 16, 2026.

7.4 Beyond-Use Dating (BUD)

The BUD is the date after which a compounded preparation should not be used. The PMS
enforces BUD assignment:

    À The default BUD is set on the formula (e.g., 180 days for non-sterile, 14 days for sterile).
    À The pharmacist may adjust the BUD at QA based on the earliest ingredient expiration or

         stability data.

À The BUD prints on the batch label and is tracked in the system for expiration alerts.

7.5 Compliance Documentation

The compounding module generates all records required for USP <795> and <797>
compliance:

    À Master Formula Record (MFR) -- The approved formula with all versions.
    À Compounding Record (CR) -- The batch record with actual quantities, lot numbers,

         compounder, verifier, and QA results.
    À Batch labels with NDC, lot, BUD, and storage conditions.
    À All records are retained in the system for the required retention period.

8. Shipping Processes

The Shipping module manages the preparation, dispatch, and tracking of prescription deliveries
to patients who cannot pick up in-store.

8.1 Creating a Shipment

    7. Navigate to Shipping > New Shipment.
    8. Select the patient and the prescriptions to include.
    9. Confirm or update the shipping address from the patient's record.
    10. Select the carrier: USPS, UPS, or FedEx.
    11. Enable cold chain tracking if the shipment contains refrigerated medications.
    12. Enable signature required if the shipment contains controlled substances or high-value

         items.
    13. Save the shipment. Status is set to Pending.

8.2 Shipment Status Lifecycle

Each shipment progresses through these stages:
    À Pending -- Shipment created, awaiting packing.
    À Packed -- Medications packed and ready for carrier pickup.
    À Shipped -- Carrier has picked up the package. Tracking number entered.
    À In Transit -- Package is in the carrier's delivery network.
    À Delivered -- Package delivered to the patient. Confirmation recorded.
    À Returned -- Package returned to pharmacy (undeliverable, refused, etc.).

  Staff Role: Shipping Clerk
  Packs shipments, enters tracking numbers, manages carrier pickups, and confirms deliveries.

8.3 Shipping Routes

The Shipping Routes page allows management of recurring delivery routes for local patients:

    À Define routes with a name, driver, and list of delivery stops.
    À Assign shipments to routes for bulk local delivery.
    À Track delivery status per stop on the route.

9. Patient Messaging & Communications

The Messaging module enables pharmacy staff to send notifications to patients via email and
SMS, manage communication preferences, and maintain a complete audit log for HIPAA
compliance.

9.1 Notification Templates

The system includes five pre-built notification templates:

    À Ready for Pickup -- Sent when a prescription enters the waiting bin. Includes patient
         name, medication, and pharmacy address.

    À Refill Due -- Sent when a patient's medication supply is running low based on days
         supply calculation.

    À Refill Processed -- Sent when a refill request has been approved and filled.
    À Shipping Update -- Sent when a shipment status changes (shipped, in transit,

         delivered).
    À Prescription Expiring -- Sent when a prescription is nearing its expiration date with no

         remaining refills.

9.2 Sending Notifications

    9. Navigate to Messaging from the sidebar.
    10. Enter the patient's ID or MRN.
    11. Select the notification template.
    12. Choose the delivery channels: Email, SMS, or both.
    13. Click Send Notification.
    14. The system sends the message through the configured email provider and/or Twilio

         SMS gateway.

  System Action (Automated)
  Automated notifications (Ready for Pickup, Refill Due, Shipping Updates) are sent automatically by
  the system based on prescription status changes. Manual notifications can also be sent by staff from
  the Messaging page.

9.3 Communication Log & Compliance

Every notification sent is logged in the Communication Log with:

    À Patient name and ID
    À Message template used
    À Channel (email/SMS)
    À Timestamp of send
